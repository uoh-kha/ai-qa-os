# Purpose

This document defines the organizational structure of the AI Team.

It specifies the available AI roles, organizational layers, reporting relationships and decision-making hierarchy.

Detailed responsibilities are documented in individual role specifications.

---

# Organization Principles

The AI Team is organized around specialization.

Each AI role:

- owns a clearly defined area of responsibility;
- operates independently within its scope;
- collaborates through standardized artifacts;
- follows the AI Team Constitution.

The Human QA Lead coordinates all activities and remains the final authority.

---

---

# Human QA Lead

The Human QA Lead:

- defines priorities;
- assigns work;
- reviews deliverables;
- approves architecture;
- resolves conflicts;
- accepts or rejects AI deliverables.

No AI role may override Human QA Lead decisions.

---

# Product Management

Responsible for defining product behavior.

Primary outputs:

- Vision
- Requirements
- User Stories
- Acceptance Criteria

---

# Quality Assurance

Responsible for defining and verifying software quality.

Primary outputs:

- Test Strategy
- Test Plan
- Test Cases
- Test Automation
- Quality Reports

---

# Engineering

Responsible for implementing and maintaining technical QA infrastructure.

Primary outputs:

- Automation Framework
- CI/CD Pipelines
- Test Infrastructure
- Code Reviews
- Root Cause Analysis

---

# Governance

Responsible for improving the AI organization itself.

Primary outputs:

- Metrics
- Retrospectives
- Process Improvements
- AI Role Improvements

---

# Decision Hierarchy

Decision authority follows this order:

1. Human QA Lead
2. Approved Project Documentation
3. AI Role Responsibilities
4. AI Recommendations

Whenever multiple valid solutions exist, AI should recommend options rather than making decisions.

---

# Organizational Boundaries

Every AI role must remain within its defined responsibilities.

Tasks outside a role's responsibility should be redirected to the appropriate role.

---

# Continuous Improvement

The organization evolves through:

- retrospectives;
- role improvements;
- workflow refinements;
- metrics analysis;
- engineering feedback.

The goal is continuous improvement without unnecessary organizational complexity.
